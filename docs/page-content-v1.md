### Section 4: Dynamic codes

> *Answers: "What if I need to change something after printing?"*

**Section headline:**
Print once. Update forever.

**Body:**
Dynamic QR codes let you change where a scan goes — anytime, from your dashboard. New seasonal menu? Updated event schedule? Different landing page for a new campaign? Change the destination. The printed code stays the same.

This is the difference between reprinting 1,000 flyers and just... not.

**How it works (simple visual):**
1. You create a QR code pointing to `yoursite.com/summer-menu`
2. You print it on table cards
3. Fall rolls around — you update the destination to `yoursite.com/fall-menu`
4. Same printed code, new destination. Zero waste.

**Callout:**
Every code on the free plan is dynamic. Because static-only free tiers aren't really free — they're demos.

### Section 6: FAQ

**Section headline:**
Questions? Good ones.

**What happens to my QR codes if I downgrade?**
They keep working. If you go from Pro to Free and have more than 15 codes, your existing codes continue to scan and redirect. You just can't create new ones until you're back under the limit. We will never deactivate codes you've already printed.

**Can I switch plans anytime?**
Yes. Upgrade or downgrade whenever you want — changes take effect immediately. If you upgrade mid-cycle, you'll be charged the prorated difference. If you downgrade, you'll keep your current plan's features until the end of your billing period.

**Is there a free trial for Pro or Business?**
You don't need one. Start with the free plan — it's genuinely useful on its own. When you need more, upgrade. There's no artificial time limit or feature lockout to pressure you.

**What counts as a "dynamic" QR code?**
A dynamic code is one where you can change the destination URL after creating it, and where scans are tracked. Every code you create on Qrius Codes is dynamic. We don't make static-only codes and call them "free."

**Do you offer refunds?**
If you're unhappy within the first 14 days of a paid plan, reach out and we'll sort it out. We're humans, not a terms-of-service wall.

**Can I use Qrius Codes for commercial purposes?**
Absolutely. Free plan included. Your codes, your business, your revenue.

**What payment methods do you accept?**
All major credit and debit cards via Stripe. We don't store your card details — Stripe handles all of that securely.

**Do you offer annual billing?**
Yes — save 22% when you pay annually. But we also offer monthly billing with no penalty, because sometimes you just want to go month by month. We get it.

**Section headline:**
The short version.

**Body:**
We needed a QR code. Simple enough. So we tried the tools that came up first on Google.

**Four values — short and direct:**

**Make the free plan actually free.**
Not a 14-day trial. Not a teaser with watermarks. A real, useful product that helps real businesses — without ever asking for a credit card. If the free plan isn't good enough to build loyalty on its own, the product isn't good enough.

And yes, we know it looks like a typo at first. That's part of the charm.

# CONTENT FLOW NOTES

## User journey mapping

**Journey 1: "I need a QR code right now"**
Google → Homepage hero → "Start creating" → Sign up → Create flow
*Fast path. Minimal friction. These people don't need convincing — they need speed.*

**Journey 2: "I'm comparing QR code tools"**
Google "[competitor] alternative" → Comparison page → Pricing → Sign up
*Data-driven. They want to see the numbers side by side. Trust signals matter.*

**Journey 3: "I'm exploring QR codes for my business"**
Google "QR codes for restaurants" → Use case page → Features → Pricing → Sign up
*Education-first. They're early in the process. Show them what's possible, then show them how easy it is.*

**Journey 4: "I've been burned by another tool"**
Google "QR code generator alternative" → Comparison page or Homepage → Trust signals (pricing transparency, codes survive cancellation) → Sign up
*Trust-first. They're skeptical. Every honesty signal matters. Don't oversell — just be straight.*

**Journey 5: "I'm already using Qrius and want to learn more"**
Dashboard → Blog / Support wiki / Changelog
*Retention and education. Help them get more value from the tool they're already using.*
