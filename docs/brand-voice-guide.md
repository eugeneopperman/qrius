# Qrius Codes — Brand Voice Guide

> The definitive reference for how Qrius Codes speaks, writes, and shows up. Every page, email, tooltip, and error message should feel like it came from the same person.

---

## Who We Are

Qrius Codes is a QR code platform for people who'd rather be running their business than fiddling with software. We make it easy to create beautiful, trackable QR codes — and we do it without the gotchas, the fine print, or the "surprise, you owe us $200" moments that plague this industry.

The name plays on "curious" — because every code is a small moment of discovery. Someone scans, something appears. It's a tiny gift, a reveal, a doorway. We like that feeling, and it shows up in how we talk.

---

## Brand Personality

**If Qrius were a person**, they'd be the friend who's genuinely good at design and tech but never makes you feel dumb for asking questions. They explain things clearly, crack the occasional joke, and somehow make the whole process feel... fun? They don't talk down to you, and they don't hide behind jargon. They're confident without being loud about it.

**Reference points:** Notion's quiet confidence + Mailchimp's warmth and wit.

## Voice Principles

### 1. Clear over clever
Clarity always comes first. We love a good turn of phrase, but never at the expense of understanding. If someone has to re-read a sentence to get it, we've failed.

| Do | Don't |
|---|---|
| "Create a QR code in under a minute." | "Leverage our proprietary generation engine for rapid code deployment." |
| "Track every scan — where, when, and what device." | "Gain granular, real-time insights into multi-dimensional scan event data." |

### 2. Warm, not wacky
We're friendly and human. We'll crack a smile, but we're not doing stand-up. Humor should feel like a wink, not a nudge in the ribs. One well-placed line beats five trying too hard.

| Do | Don't |
|---|---|
| "Your QR code, but make it gorgeous." | "OMG your QR codes are about to be FIRE!!!" |
| "No credit card. No catch. Seriously." | "FREE FREE FREE! What are you waiting for?!" |

### 3. Confident, not cocky
We know our product is good. We can say so without tearing competitors down by name or making promises we can't keep. Let the value speak — then give it a mic.

| Do | Don't |
|---|---|
| "5 free dynamic QR codes. Most tools give you one or two." | "Unlike [Competitor], we don't rip you off." |
| "Built for teams that move fast." | "The world's most advanced QR platform." |

### 4. Honest, always
The QR code industry has a trust problem — bait-and-switch pricing, codes that die when you cancel, surprise annual charges. We're the antidote. Say what we mean. Mean what we say. If something has a limit, say so upfront.

| Do | Don't |
|---|---|
| "Free plan includes 5 dynamic codes with unlimited scans." | "Unlimited free codes!*" *(asterisk buried in ToS)* |
| "Starter begins at $12/month, billed monthly. Cancel anytime." | "Starting at just $12" *(but actually $120 upfront, annual only)* |

### 5. Guiding, not gatekeeping
We're the friendly guide. We assume our reader is smart but maybe new to QR codes. We explain without over-explaining. We show the way without being patronizing.

| Do | Don't |
|---|---|
| "Dynamic codes let you change where a scan goes — even after you've printed it." | "Dynamic QR codes utilize URL redirect technology to enable post-deployment destination modification." |
| "Not sure which type to pick? Start with URL — it works for almost everything." | "Select the appropriate QR taxonomy for your use case." |

## Tone Spectrum

Our voice stays the same, but the tone flexes depending on context:

| Context | Tone | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **Homepage / Marketing** | Confident, inviting, a touch playful | "Beautiful QR codes that actually get scanned." |
| **Product UI / Tooltips** | Helpful, concise, encouraging | "Nice — your code is ready to download." |
| **Onboarding** | Warm, patient, guiding | "Let's set up your first QR code. It takes about 30 seconds." |
| **Pricing page** | Transparent, direct, reassuring | "No hidden fees. No annual lock-in. Cancel whenever." |
| **Error messages** | Calm, human, solution-oriented | "That didn't work — try a shorter URL or check for typos." |
| **Blog / Education** | Knowledgeable, conversational, practical | "Here's what most people get wrong about QR code colors." |
| **Legal pages** | Plain language where possible, straightforward | "We don't sell your data. Here's exactly what we collect and why." |
| **Changelog** | Excited but grounded, specific | "You can now export to SVG on the free plan. You asked, we built it." |

## Writing Style Rules

### Sentence structure
- Short sentences. Medium sentences are fine too. But if a sentence needs a second comma, consider splitting it.
- Lead with the benefit, not the feature. "Track every scan in real time" before "Built-in analytics dashboard."
- Use the active voice. "You create" not "codes are created."

### Vocabulary
- Use everyday words. "Use" not "utilize." "Start" not "initiate." "Change" not "modify."
- Say "QR code" in full on first mention per page. "Code" is fine after that.
- "You" and "your" — we're always talking to someone, not about someone.

### Punctuation & formatting
- Oxford comma: yes.
- Exclamation marks: one per page, maximum. Earn it.
- Em dashes — great for asides and emphasis. Use freely.
- Ellipsis: sparingly, and only for genuine trailing-off moments.
- Headlines: sentence case ("Create your first QR code"), not title case ("Create Your First QR Code").

### Humor guidelines
- Humor should be a seasoning, not the main dish.
- Best places for it: headlines, subheadings, empty states, 404 pages, CTAs.
- Worst places for it: error messages with real consequences, pricing details, legal copy.
- If a joke needs explaining, cut it.
- Self-aware > sarcastic. Playful > punny.

### Numbers & data
- Use specific numbers when they make us look good. "5 free dynamic codes" is better than "a generous free plan."
- Compare to industry norms when the gap is dramatic. "Most tools give you 1-3 free codes. We give you 5."
- Don't inflate. If something is fast, say how fast. If it's easy, show the steps.

## The Qrius Discovery Thread

The name "Qrius" gives us a recurring creative motif: **curiosity and discovery**. Use it sparingly — it's most powerful when it shows up unexpectedly.

**Good uses:**
- "Stay qrius." (sign-off, footer, email closer)
- "What will they discover?" (when talking about what happens after a scan)
- "Every scan is a little moment of discovery."
- Section headers that play on curiosity: "Qrius about analytics?" / "Get qrius with your brand."

**Overuse to avoid:**
- Don't force the pun into every headline.
- Don't use "qrius" as a replacement for "curious" in body copy — it reads as a typo.
- One "qrius" wordplay moment per page is the max. Zero is also fine.
