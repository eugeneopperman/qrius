Your Qrius Codes dashboard is the hub for managing, tracking, and organizing all your QR codes. Let's walk through the key sections.

## Sidebar Navigation

On the left side of your dashboard, you'll find:

**Home**
Main dashboard overview with quick stats and recent activity.

**QR Codes**
View all your QR codes in a searchable, filterable list.

**Create**
Launch the 4-step wizard to generate a new QR code (also available via the big orange button).

**Templates**
Save and reuse custom branding styles—colors, logos, frames—so you don't rebuild designs from scratch.

**Settings**
Manage your profile, team, billing, API keys, and security.

## Quick Stats

At the top of your dashboard, you'll see four key metrics:

- **Total QR Codes** — How many you've created (your plan limit shown)
- **Scans This Month** — Real-time scan count
- **Team Members** — How many people have access to your account
- **API Requests** — If you're on Pro or Business, your API usage this month

These update in real-time as your QR codes get scanned.

## QR Code List

Below the stats, your QR codes are displayed as cards or rows (toggle between views with the grid/list icon).

### Card View (Default)

Each card shows:
- **Thumbnail preview** of your QR code (with any custom colors, logo, frame you designed)
- **Campaign name** (or auto-generated name if you didn't give it one)
- **QR type badge** (URL, WiFi, Text, etc.)
- **Status indicator** (Active, Draft, or Paused)
- **Quick actions** (menu with edit, copy link, delete, etc.)

### List View

Condensed rows with:
- QR code thumbnail (smaller)
- Campaign name
- Type and status
- Creation date
- Scan count
- Inline action buttons

## Filters & Search

**Search Box**
Type a campaign name, URL, or keyword to instantly find your QR code.

**Status Tabs**
Filter by **All**, **Active**, **Draft**, or **Paused** codes. Draft QR codes are work-in-progress and don't track scans.

**Folders**
Organize your codes into custom folders (e.g., "Marketing 2025", "Social Media", "Events"). Click a folder to filter the list.

**Sort & View**
Sort by newest, oldest, most scanned, or alphabetical. Toggle between card and list views.

## Creating a New QR Code

Click the orange **Create** button (top-right, or in the sidebar) to launch the wizard. You can also click **+ New** from within any folder.

## Editing & Managing QR Codes

### Quick Actions

Hover over a QR code card or click the three-dot menu to:

- **Edit** — Change the destination URL (for URL-type codes)
- **Copy Link** — Get the tracking link to share
- **Download** — Re-download in PNG, SVG, or PDF
- **View Analytics** — See scans, geolocation, device breakdowns
- **Pause/Resume** — Temporarily turn off tracking
- **Delete** — Permanently remove the code

### Edit Destination URL

For URL-type QR codes, you can update where the code points without changing the QR image itself. This is the power of dynamic codes—adjust your campaigns on the fly.

## Folders

Organize your QR codes into folders by clicking **New Folder** or right-clicking a code to move it.

**Folder actions:**
- Create a new folder
- Rename an existing folder
- Move codes in and out
- Delete a folder (codes stay in your account, just lose the folder)

## Analytics

Click any QR code to open its **detail page**. Here you'll find:

- **Total scans** over time (daily and hourly charts)
- **Geography** — Which countries and regions your code was scanned
- **Technology** — Browser, OS, and device breakdown
- **Sources** — Referrer and click-through data

All analytics are collected automatically—no setup needed. Scans are tracked for:
- Free plan: 7 days
- Starter: 90 days
- Pro: 1 year
- Business: Unlimited history

## Team Members

If you're on a paid plan, you can invite team members to collaborate. They can create, edit, and track QR codes together. Manage team members and permissions in **Settings > Team**.

## Mobile Dashboard

The dashboard is fully responsive. On mobile, the sidebar collapses into a bottom navigation bar, and QR code cards stack vertically for easy scrolling.

## Pro Tips

1. **Use campaign names** — Instead of "https://example.com/sale-2025", name it "Black Friday Campaign". It's easier to spot in your list.

2. **Organize with folders** — Group codes by marketing channel, event, or date for faster navigation.

3. **Check analytics weekly** — See what's working and adjust your strategy.

4. **Duplicate successful designs** — In the Templates section, save a design you love and reuse it across campaigns.

5. **Keyboard shortcuts** — Press `?` to see all shortcuts (Ctrl+S to download, Ctrl+C to copy link, etc.).
