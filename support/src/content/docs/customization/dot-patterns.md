QR codes are made of small squares (modules) arranged in a grid. You can change how those modules look to match your brand style—from sharp geometric patterns to smooth, rounded designs.

## Dot Styles

The **dot pattern** controls how the main QR code data appears. Choose from six distinct styles:

1. **Square** - The classic QR look. Sharp 90° corners, maximum contrast.
2. **Dots** - Circles instead of squares. Softer, more approachable feel.
3. **Rounded** - Squares with slightly rounded corners. A balanced middle ground.
4. **Extra Rounded** - Heavily rounded, almost like a retro arcade aesthetic.
5. **Classy** - Geometric pattern with alternating sizes and orientations.
6. **Classy Rounded** - Same as Classy, but with rounded edges for a softer look.

To change the dot style:
1. Open the **Customize panel** (right-side tabs).
2. Click the **Style tab** (the pattern icon).
3. **Select a dot style** from the previews.
4. Watch the QR code update in real-time.

**Best for scannability:** Square and Dots are the most reliable. Rounded and Extra Rounded work well but test on older phones. Classy patterns are artistic but may reduce scan speed on some devices.

## Corner Styles

The three corner squares (position markers) help phones recognize and orient the QR code. Style these independently:

- **Corner Square Type:** The large corner boxes.
  - Square, Rounded, Extra Rounded versions available.
- **Corner Dot Type:** The small dots inside the corner squares.
  - Square, Dots, Rounded, Extra Rounded options.

These can be different from your main dot style, giving you creative freedom. For example:
- Main dots: Dots (circles)
- Corner squares: Rounded
- Corner dots: Extra Rounded

## Corner Softness (Roundness Slider)

Fine-tune how rounded the corners are with the **Roundness slider** (0–100%):
- **0%:** Fully sharp corners (geometric).
- **50%:** Balanced roundness.
- **100%:** Maximum curvature (looks almost organic).

This applies globally to both dot patterns and corner styles, so you can dial in exactly the look you want without picking a predefined style.

## Combining Patterns

Your dot style, corner square style, and corner dot style can all be different. Experiment:

**Minimalist brand:**
- Dots: Dots (circles)
- Corner squares: Rounded
- Corner dots: Dots
- Roundness: 40%

**Bold statement:**
- Dots: Classy Rounded
- Corner squares: Rounded
- Corner dots: Extra Rounded
- Roundness: 80%

## Testing Scannability

After choosing your pattern:
1. **Download the QR code** (PNG or SVG).
2. **Test on 2–3 phones** with different camera apps.
3. **Check at different sizes** (small stickers, large posters).
4. **Scan from various distances** and angles.

If a pattern consistently fails to scan, return to Square or Dots—they're the most universally reliable.

Your pattern choices are saved with your QR code. When you apply a brand template or export the style, all pattern settings travel with it.
